Commercial cleaning FAQs

Can cleaning happen outside business hours?

Yes. Most commercial clients prefer early morning or after-hours cleaning so the work does not interrupt staff or visitors. We can discuss access and timing when preparing the quote.

Do you offer one-off commercial cleans?

Yes. We can quote one-off cleans for handovers, inspections, events, pre-opening refreshes, or spaces that need a reset before moving to a regular schedule.

How do you quote regular office cleaning?

We look at the site size, number of work areas, amenities, frequency, access, and the level of detail required. A short description or walkthrough is usually enough to start.

Regular office cleaning scope

A useful commercial cleaning plan separates the everyday tasks from the periodic detail work. That keeps the routine clear, avoids missed expectations, and makes it easier to adjust the service as your workplace changes.

Every visit can include

These are the routine tasks most businesses expect in a regular commercial clean.

  • Emptying bins, replacing liners, and removing general rubbish from agreed areas.
  • Wiping desks, counters, meeting tables, reception surfaces, and high-touch points.
  • Cleaning kitchen benches, sinks, splashbacks, appliance fronts, and staff room surfaces.
  • Cleaning amenities, mirrors, basins, door handles, dispensers, and visible touchpoints.
  • Vacuuming carpeted areas and mopping hard floors where included in the scope.

Periodic detail work

Some work is better scheduled weekly, monthly, or as needed rather than forced into every visit.

  • Detailed dusting of skirting boards, ledges, vents, picture frames, and low-use surfaces.
  • Extra attention to meeting rooms, boardrooms, training rooms, and shared staff spaces.
  • Spot cleaning marks on glass, doors, light switches, walls, and cupboard fronts.
  • Periodic hard-floor care, internal glass cleaning, or deeper amenity refreshes.
  • Seasonal resets before inspections, audits, client visits, or office changes.
