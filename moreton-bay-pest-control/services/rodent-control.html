Exterior control

Exterior and rodent control for property edges

Many pest problems start outside before they move indoors. This page covers perimeter treatments, entry point advice and practical rodent prevention notes.

Keeping pests outside

Exterior pest control focuses on the places pests travel, shelter and enter: wall edges, gardens, patios, bins, garages, sheds, roof lines and small openings around the property.

For rodent concerns, clear advice matters. Customers need to know where activity has been seen, how food or storage may contribute, and what access is needed for safe placement and follow-up.

Common focus areas

  • Perimeter treatments around external walls and entry points
  • Garages, sheds, bins, storage zones and ceiling access notes
  • Covered bait station planning where appropriate
  • Prevention advice for gaps, food sources and cluttered storage

Best suited for

Homes seeing pests around exterior walls, garages or storage areas, or signs of rodent movement around sheds, bins and roof spaces.

Perimeter pressure

Ants, spiders and cockroaches often move through exterior cracks, doors, drains, garden beds and shaded edges before appearing inside.

Rodent signs

Droppings, scratching sounds, chewed packaging, greasy rub marks and movement near bins or storage areas should be described in the enquiry.

Prevention

Sealing gaps, improving storage, trimming vegetation and managing food sources can support the treatment and reduce repeat pressure.
