Industry-specific cleaning considerations

The same cleaning checklist does not suit every business. A useful scope should reflect who uses the space, how often surfaces are touched, what visitors see first, and what needs to be ready before doors open.

Customer-facing workplaces

Offices, clinics, showrooms, and reception areas need consistent presentation because clients and visitors notice small details quickly.

  • Reception counters, entry doors, waiting areas, meeting rooms, and visible high-touch surfaces.
  • Clean floors, glass, bins, amenities, and staff kitchens before customers or patients arrive.
  • Quiet scheduling around business hours, appointments, trading times, and staff movement.
  • Periodic detail cleaning before inspections, audits, client visits, or seasonal peaks.

Operational and shared facilities

Warehouses, education spaces, body corporate common areas, and staff facilities need practical routines that stand up to repeat use.

  • Shared entries, corridors, stairwells, lift areas, bin rooms, staff rooms, and amenities.
  • High-use touchpoints such as handles, rails, switches, tables, counters, and kitchen surfaces.
  • Clear expectations for access, security, keys, parking, alarms, and after-hours cleaning.
  • Flexible scheduling when site traffic, tenant use, school terms, or operating hours change.

What we need to understand your site

For a useful first quote, send the practical details that shape the cleaning routine. This helps avoid a generic price that does not match the way your site actually operates.

  • Industry type and whether the space is client-facing, staff-only, shared, or mixed-use.
  • Approximate size, number of rooms, amenities, kitchens, entries, and common areas.
  • Current cleaning frequency and what is not working with the existing routine.
  • Preferred timing, access rules, building security, parking, and after-hours requirements.
  • Any floor care, window cleaning, high-touch cleaning, or one-off detail work required.
  • Upcoming inspections, audits, lease handovers, busy periods, or presentation deadlines.
