First Home Buyers

Plain-English guidance for first-home buyers working through deposits, grants, schemes, lender criteria and the steps between pre-approval and settlement.

What we help with

Buying a first home can feel complicated because several decisions happen at once. We help organise the process so buyers can understand borrowing power, upfront costs and lender expectations before making offers.

Common inclusions

  • Deposit and genuine savings check
  • First-home grant and scheme discussion where relevant
  • Pre-approval pathway and document checklist
  • Offer, contract and settlement step guidance

How the process usually works

Understand the goal

We start with the reason for the loan, timing, property plans and the outcome you want.

Check the numbers

Income, expenses, deposit, equity and debts are reviewed before any lender path is suggested.

Compare suitable options

We compare lender policy, features, fees and likely fit instead of focusing only on headline rates.

Support the application

We help organise documents, lodge the application and keep the next step clear through approval.

Best suited to

  • First-time buyers unsure where to start
  • Buyers comparing apartments, townhouses or houses
  • Couples or families wanting a realistic purchase range

Bring to the first chat

  • Savings history
  • Employment and income details
  • Rough purchase goal
  • Any questions about grants, guarantees or upfront costs
