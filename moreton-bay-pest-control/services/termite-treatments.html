Termite treatments

Termite treatment plans with clear next steps

When termites are confirmed, the page should explain treatment planning without panic: what is known, what needs access, and how follow-up protection is managed.

Treatment planning

The right termite treatment depends on the species, activity, building type, moisture conditions and whether the goal is active treatment, barrier work, monitoring or a staged plan.

This sample page gives the future buyer a clear service structure they can adapt once their licensing, warranty terms and treatment methods are confirmed.

Typical discussion points

  • Inspection findings and areas of active termite evidence
  • Recommended treatment method and why it suits the property
  • Access, drilling, station placement or monitoring requirements
  • Follow-up visits, reporting and prevention advice

Best suited for

Homes with confirmed termite activity, inspection findings that need action, or previous termite issues that need ongoing monitoring.

Discuss treatment

Confirmed activity

Treatment pages should avoid vague promises. They should explain that proper recommendations follow inspection findings and site conditions.

Access and preparation

Customers may need to clear storage, provide access to garages or gardens, and understand which areas must remain undisturbed.

Follow-up protection

Termite management is rarely a one-off conversation. Monitoring, maintenance and future inspections should be part of the service path.
