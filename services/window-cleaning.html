Window cleaning scope in more detail

Commercial glass is not just the outside windows. Entries, internal partitions, reception glass, display windows, and door panels all affect how clean the premises feels.

Internal glass

Internal glass often collects fingerprints, hand marks, dust, and smears that are easy for staff and visitors to notice.

  • Reception glass, office partitions, meeting room panels, and glass doors.
  • Fingerprints, smudges, dust, surface marks, and visible hand-contact areas.
  • Spot cleaning between scheduled services for high-use entry and reception glass.
  • Work that can be added to regular office cleaning where suitable.

External and customer-facing glass

External windows and shopfronts need a practical plan around access, exposure, safety, and opening hours.

  • Shopfront windows, entry doors, low-level external glass, and accessible office windows.
  • Street dust, water marks, fingerprints, cobwebs, and traffic film on visible glass.
  • Early morning or after-hours timing for retail and customer-facing premises.
  • Frequency options for weekly, fortnightly, monthly, or seasonal presentation cleans.

What helps us quote window cleaning

Good access details help us give a clearer quote and avoid promising work that cannot be done safely or practically.

  • Number of windows, doors, partitions, or glass panels.
  • Whether the work is internal, external, or both.
  • Photos of the glass areas and any height or access restrictions.
  • Preferred timing, especially for shopfronts or customer-facing premises.
  • Whether the glass needs a one-off clean or ongoing presentation care.
  • Nearby parking, after-hours access, and any building rules we should know.

Window cleaning FAQs

Can you clean internal glass partitions?

Yes. Internal glass, meeting room panels, reception glass, and door panels can be included in the scope.

Do you clean shopfront windows?

Yes. Shopfront and customer-facing windows are a common request, especially for retail and showroom sites.

How often should business windows be cleaned?

It depends on exposure and foot traffic. High-visibility sites often need more frequent cleaning than back-office or low-traffic premises.
