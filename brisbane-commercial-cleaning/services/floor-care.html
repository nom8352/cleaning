Floor care services

A good floor care quote starts with the surface. Vinyl, tile, polished concrete, sealed floors, and hard commercial surfaces all need a slightly different approach.

Machine scrubbing

For hard floors that need more than a mop, machine scrubbing helps lift built-up marks, traffic film, and everyday soil.

Polishing and presentation

Periodic polishing can help restore a cleaner finish and improve the way lobbies, corridors, and customer-facing areas present.

Stripping and sealing

Where suitable, stripping and sealing can help protect the floor surface and make regular maintenance easier.

Maintenance planning

We can recommend a sensible rhythm for high-traffic spaces so floor care is planned rather than left until it looks worn.

Floor care scope in more detail

A floor care job needs more detail than a standard clean because the method depends on the surface, age, wear, previous seal, foot traffic, and how soon the area needs to reopen.

Surface and traffic checks

Before quoting, we look at what the floor is made from and how it is used day to day.

  • Hard floor type, including vinyl, tile, sealed concrete, polished concrete, or other commercial surfaces.
  • High-traffic zones such as entries, walkways, counters, corridors, kitchens, and waiting areas.
  • Visible scuffs, dull patches, worn edges, sticky build-up, staining, and older seal condition.
  • Access, furniture movement, drying time, and whether work needs to happen after hours.

Planned floor maintenance

Not every floor needs the same treatment. We help separate routine cleaning from periodic care.

  • Machine scrubbing for floors that need a stronger clean than routine mopping.
  • Buffing or polishing to improve presentation in lobbies, retail areas, and customer-facing zones.
  • Stripping and sealing where suitable for the floor and the condition of the existing finish.
  • Maintenance schedules for sites that want floors to stay presentable between larger services.

What helps us quote floor care

The more practical detail you can share, the easier it is to recommend the right method and avoid quoting work the floor does not need.

  • Approximate floor area or number of rooms.
  • Floor type and whether it has been sealed before.
  • Photos of dull areas, worn patches, stains, or heavy traffic marks.
  • Preferred timing, access arrangements, and whether the space can be left to dry.
  • Furniture, shelving, stock, or equipment that may need to be worked around.
  • Whether this is a one-off refresh or part of an ongoing maintenance plan.

Floor care FAQs

How often should commercial floors be polished?

It depends on the surface and traffic. Some sites need periodic attention every few months, while lower-traffic areas may only need occasional work.

Can floor care be done after hours?

Yes. Floor work is often best done outside trading or office hours so the area can be cleaned, dried, and ready for use.

Do you need to inspect the floor first?

For most floor care jobs, a quick site check or clear photos help us quote more accurately and recommend the right method.
