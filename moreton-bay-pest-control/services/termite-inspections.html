Termite inspections

Clear termite inspections before the damage spreads

A termite inspection should be calm, methodical and easy to understand. This page explains what is checked, what helps access and how findings can be reported.

Inspection scope

Moreton Bay properties can have varied termite risk depending on moisture, landscaping, stored timber, slab edges, subfloor access and nearby vegetation.

The inspection copy should reassure the customer without using scare tactics. It should explain that the goal is to identify visible evidence, risk conditions and sensible next steps.

Common inspection points

  • Accessible internal rooms, skirting boards and wet areas
  • Roof voids, garages, fences, garden edges and stored timber
  • Moisture-prone zones and visible timber-contact risks
  • Plain-English findings, photos and practical recommendations

Best suited for

Annual checks, property buyers, landlords, homeowners seeing timber damage, or anyone unsure whether the risk is active.

Before the inspection

Make garages, roof access, subfloor entries and wall edges as accessible as possible. Mention previous termite work or moisture issues.

During the visit

The technician checks accessible areas, looks for evidence and risk conditions, and explains any areas that could not be inspected.

After the report

Findings should separate urgent treatment needs from maintenance advice, monitoring options and longer-term prevention steps.
